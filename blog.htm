Why I Left L&T – The Untold Truth Behind My Exit

By Jugal Pakhare

Ever since my departure from L&T, many created rumors and their own theories. But today, I want to share the truth. L&T is one of the biggest firms in India, and thousands of engineers dream of getting placed there. But dreams change when reality hits hard.

1) No Room for Higher Studies

L&T doesn’t allow DETs to pursue full-time higher studies. Even after training, company sponsorship depends on your ratings and manager’s recommendation. That too only for part-time degrees from tier-1 colleges — but I haven’t met a single employee who actually got that.

Diploma is no longer sufficient in today’s competitive world. A degree is almost mandatory for better jobs or competitive exams. Company policy does allow employees to pursue part-time degrees, but such degrees are less valuable and difficult to manage due to 12-hour shifts and no proper leave system.

2) No Scope for Promotion

Promotion depends entirely on your supervisor’s recommendation, not merit. Even after getting promoted from DET, your salary increases by just ₹5–10K. DETs struggle more than GETs to rise, and the company’s promise of “DET to MD” seems like a hoax on the ground.

3) Worst Working Culture

L&T construction sites are a perfect example of poor labor law enforcement. 12-hour shifts, no weekly holidays, and zero flexibility on public or religious holidays. Holidays are given only when workers themselves refuse to work, like on Holi.

Even then, EHS staff must patrol and monitor sites. It was exhausting, both mentally and physically.

4) Lack of Professionalism

Your cadre defines your respect level. As a trainee, I was treated like a petty worker. When I reported issues to my senior, I was told not to bother him due to my “lower cadre.” Calling someone “Sharma ji” led to a shouting session — I was ordered to say “Sharma Sir” because I was junior. Departments often fought among themselves in WhatsApp groups — a serious lack of coordination and teamwork.

5) Worst Working Environment

My site had no clean drinking water, no toilets, no meals — not even tea. In night shifts, no dinner breaks. Vehicles promised in offer letters never showed up. Water came from dirty bores, never tested by EHS. Workers toiled under 45°C with no shade. Complaints went unanswered. This wasn’t temporary — it went on for 2 years straight.

6) Lack of Future Scope

Even after promotion, your job, environment, and work shifts do not change. You can’t switch roles easily. There’s no long-term vision or path laid down for true career growth or self-development.

Gratitude and Closure

This doesn’t mean L&T gave me only pain. I’m thankful for the real-world experience and lessons I gained. I’ll soon publish a detailed blog about my journey — from joining, orientation, site life to resignation. But for now, this is about the reasons behind my exit.

There are other personal and situational reasons too, but I’ll share them when the time is right.

Conclusion

To those who look up to corporate life, remember: Not every big brand gives you a big life. Your peace, growth, and dreams matter more. Thanks for reading. More coming soon.

— Jugal Pakhare
